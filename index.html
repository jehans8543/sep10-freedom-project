Government




Context

What is the government? When you think about it may seem like an easy question to answer. Most typically saying, “The government is a group of people who run the country” or “They make laws”. All of these are simple, but yet factually correct answers. Most of them being universally correct answers. Though if you wanted to have a more complex answer, it would vary depending on the person and region. The reason for this being that there are different types of governments all around the world. Such as a democracy, in which the people have the power. There’s monarchies with their country being ruled by a king or queen. Then we have authoritarianism with the power being held by one person or a small group. We then have a republic, with the citizens having the ability to elect their representative. There’s also a theocracy, a nation being ruled by religious law. As you can see all these types of governments vary from one another to a different extent. Therefore, changing the perspective and belief of people who give an in-depth answer as to what the government is. Despite the differences, there is something that every government around the world can commonly agree on. They exist to create order, manage society, and maintain control—regardless of who’s in charge or how they got there. In order for them to achieve this they use technology such as softwares and hardwares to make that job more efficient for them.

The softwares OpenGov, Oracle, and Accela are all considered to be crucial resources used in the government for numerous reasons. They all give governments real-time access to data, dashboards, and reports, allowing them to make faster decisions based on accurate information. With the use of these softwares the government was also able to catch public trust and transparency, aligning with the citizens appeal. These platforms make financial data, performance metrics, and project updates available to the public, building that trust and accountability. Not only do they upload their data, but they also make it available for the public to use such as online permits, licenses, or budget participation. Improving convenience for citizens and reduces in-person workload. Without these platforms our governments would be less efficient, transparent, and less capable of meeting the needs of modern citizens.



Hardwares


Physical Access Control Systems (PACS), Information Technology (IT) infrastructure, and Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) are all directly correlated with the government responsibility to protect people, information, and infrastructure. They are all considered to be essential government hardware components because they protect the core physical and digital infrastructure that governments rely on to function safely, securely, and efficiently.


  • Enterprise intelligence systems of hardware and software deployed to control accesses through perimeter entries, internal control points, and protected spaces.
  • Include, but are not limited to, Personal Identity Verification card readers, door controllers, turnstiles, request to exit devices, door position switches/sensors, electric door strikes, Fiber Optic cabling infrastructure, middleware, switches, and servers.

  • Central to national security, economy, public health, and etc.
  • Produces and provides hardware, software, and information technology systems and services.

  • Programmed to detect intrusion into certain protected spaces, such as facility perimeter spaces (perimeter access locations like doors, windows, roof hatches, etc.)
  • IDS communicate alarms to monitoring stations to signal for the appropriate response to the alarmed location.
